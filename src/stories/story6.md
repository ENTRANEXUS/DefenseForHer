A 35-year-old woman from West India has been arrested after her husband allegedly died during an incident in which she kicked him in the groin while defending herself from what she described as a forced sexual assault.

The woman, identified as Rohini Kumar (name changed), told police that her 45-year-old husband, Arun Kumar (name changed), who was reportedly HIV-positive, attempted to force himself on her despite her repeated refusal. Fearing she could contract the infection, she said she acted in self-defense.

According to investigators, the incident took place at approximately 2:30 a.m. on Sunday. Police said Arun, who was allegedly intoxicated, woke his wife and demanded sexual intimacy. She refused and repeatedly asked him to stop, but he allegedly ignored her objections and continued trying to force himself on her.

As the struggle intensified, Rohini said she believed she was in immediate danger and reacted instinctively to protect herself. She kicked her husband multiple times in an effort to stop the assault, causing him to collapse. Emergency assistance was sought, but he was pronounced dead.

Police have launched an investigation into the circumstances surrounding the incident. Authorities are examining forensic evidence, witness statements, and medical reports to determine the exact sequence of events and whether the woman's actions fall within the legal scope of self-defense.
